## 4. Sustainability & Accessibility Commitments

### Sustainability

| Topic | Verified information and product implication |
|---|---|
| Carbon strategy | FIFA's published strategy language for recent tournaments emphasizes measuring, reducing, and offsetting emissions, but public reporting around 2026 has drawn criticism because long inter-city travel and a 48-team format increase aviation emissions. Treat carbon-neutral claims as requiring source-level verification before using in public-facing app copy. |
| Waste reduction | FIFA and host-city sustainability plans generally reference recycling, responsible sourcing, and waste diversion; venue-specific World Cup waste targets were not consistently published in one accessible public source. App implication: track refill stations, recycling points, and food-waste diversion as venue-configurable data rather than assuming a universal target. |
| Heat and water | Public reporting in 2026 noted concern over bottle policies and heat risk; FIFA reportedly adopted mandatory hydration breaks for matches. Fan-facing operations should surface shaded routes, water points, and medical-alert escalation. |
| Transit mode shift | Host-city plans emphasize transit, shuttle, and park-and-ride options, especially at urban venues. App implication: crowd guidance should prefer official routes and dynamically de-emphasize overloaded stations or gates. |
